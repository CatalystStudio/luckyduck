**Date:** 2026-04-07  
**Prepared by:** CRO, Executive Growth & Revenue

## Commercial framing
LuckyDuck has a credible wedge in live-event lead capture for tradeshow exhibitors, field marketing teams, and activation agencies. The product solves an operational pain that is real, visible, and expensive in lost follow-up quality: paper fishbowls, generic forms, weak branding, unreliable booth workflows, and messy winner selection.

For this forecast, I used the implemented pricing logic you provided:
- **Pro:** $49/month
- **Event:** $99/month

### Forecast assumptions
- Starting point: **10 paying customers in Month 1**
- Customer growth: **20% month-over-month**
- Plan mix: **70% Pro / 30% Event**
- Blended ARPA: **$64/month**
- Forecast is shown before applying churn; churn risk is addressed separately below

## Churn Risk Analysis
### 1. Event seasonality is the biggest commercial risk
LuckyDuck is tied to event calendars. Many customers will feel intense value immediately before and during a show, then question the subscription in off-months.

### 2. Single-event churn risk is high for exhibitors
A small exhibitor may sign up for one event, export leads, pick a winner, and cancel unless LuckyDuck becomes part of a repeat event workflow.

### 3. Agency retention should be materially better
Agencies managing multiple clients or recurring activations have stronger continuity and higher operational switching costs. This is the most attractive segment for durable revenue.

### 4. Current pricing may under-monetize high-value users
At $99/month, a high-volume event customer still may be cheap relative to the value of even a few qualified leads captured at a tradeshow. That helps adoption, but it may cap upside unless multi-brand, agency, or enterprise packaging is added.

### Seasonality impact
The raw forecast above assumes uninterrupted net growth. In reality, expect:
- weaker retention after major event cycles end
- softer summer/holiday periods depending on vertical
- temporary pauses rather than pure logo churn for some customers

Commercially, that means LuckyDuck should plan around **seasonal contraction risk in SMB exhibitors** and build packaging that preserves accounts between events.

## Recommended GTM Motion
## Recommendation: **Hybrid motion, not PLG-only**
LuckyDuck should use:
- **PLG/self-serve-assisted acquisition** for individual exhibitors and small field teams
- **Light outbound / founder-led sales** for agencies, multi-location brands, and event marketing operators

### Why not PLG only?
PLG can work for the simple booth-level use case, but it leaves too much money on the table in the agency segment, where one account could represent multiple end-clients and recurring usage across many events.

### Best near-term motion
1. **PLG-assisted funnel**
   - free beta or trial
   - fast onboarding
   - QR/demo-led conversion
   - clear “replace fishbowls and forms” positioning

2. **Targeted outbound to agencies**
   - event marketing agencies
   - experiential agencies
   - franchise/local activation operators
   - medtech / industrial exhibitors with recurring show schedules

3. **Founder-led conversion for higher-value accounts**
   - agency onboarding
   - multi-event package discussions
   - annual commitments
   - roadmap-led upsell into integrations and admin controls

### Revenue view
The highest-LTV path is likely **agency and multi-event operator accounts**, not one-off exhibitors.

## Commercial blind spots
- No clear annual packaging yet, which increases month-to-month churn exposure
- No explicit pause/retain mechanism for off-season customers
- No multi-brand or agency-native pricing in the implemented matrix yet
- No integration-led retention layer yet, which weakens stickiness after lead export
- No enterprise tier in current live pricing, despite a credible agency/field-team use case

## One specific recommendation to increase LTV
**Launch an annual Agency plan with multi-brand management, reusable event templates, and CRM export/integration support.**

Why this matters:
- it converts LuckyDuck from a one-event utility into an operating system for recurring activations
- it shifts buyers from exhibitors with episodic demand to agencies with portfolio demand
- it creates a stronger retention anchor between event cycles
- it gives the business a path to materially higher ARPA than $49 to $99

## Bottom line
LuckyDuck’s current pricing can support early adoption, but on its own it risks building a customer base with meaningful post-event churn. The right move is a **hybrid GTM model**: let smaller exhibitors convert through a product-led path, while actively selling agencies and repeat-event operators into higher-retention, higher-LTV contracts.
